# Enterprise Documentation Checklist

## Purpose

Evaluate documentation for completeness, consistency, traceability, and long-term maintainability.

## Scope

Applies to Markdown documents, decision records, runbooks, standards, guides, and change records.

## Checklist

- [ ] The title, purpose, scope, owner, status, and version metadata are present where required.
- [ ] The intended audience and authoritative role of the document are clear.
- [ ] Headings are unique, ordered, and descriptive.
- [ ] Terminology matches the approved glossary or is defined locally.
- [ ] Unknown information uses the approved placeholder convention.
- [ ] Statements distinguish approved decisions, proposals, current state, and future work.
- [ ] Internal and external references resolve to the intended targets.
- [ ] Related documents are linked without creating ambiguous authority.
- [ ] Repeated content is replaced by a canonical reference when practical.
- [ ] Examples, commands, and paths are accurate for their stated context.
- [ ] Sensitive, confidential, personal, or secret information is absent.
- [ ] The last-updated value and change history reflect the document state.

## Pass Criteria

The document is accurate, navigable, traceable, consistently formatted, and maintainable by its intended audience.

## Fail Criteria

The review fails when content is misleading, contradictory, untraceable, structurally incomplete, insecure, or dependent on broken references.
