## Purpose

Define a consistent verbal and visual character for future Damavand experiences without inventing claims, final copy, design tokens, or production content.

## Brand Personality

| Trait | Expression | Avoid |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Precise | Clear labels, explicit states, measured hierarchy | Vague superlatives or unsupported certainty |
| Dependable | Predictable patterns, visible ownership, factual evidence | Urgency tricks or unstable novelty |
| Experienced | Domain-aware structure and terminology | Unapproved technical claims |
| Premium | Restraint, finish, proportion, and detail | Decorative excess or luxury clichés |
| Direct | Short routes to products, knowledge, support, and inquiry | Dense promotional detours |
| Human | Respectful, service-oriented Persian communication | Robotic or overfamiliar language |

## Tone

- Professional, composed, concise, and helpful.
- Confident only where evidence supports confidence.
- Technical terms remain consistent with the approved glossary and product sources.
- Inquiry language explains the next step without implying an instant quote, stock guarantee, or public price.
- Error and empty-state language is actionable and non-blaming.

Exact slogans, campaign copy, claims, and legal language remain outside this document and require their own approval.

## Visual Mood

The visual mood combines structural clarity with restrained depth. Suitable cues include disciplined grids, generous breathing room, high legibility, precise edges, purposeful contrast, and selectively reflective or luminous accents.

The system must not define actual steel properties through decorative color or texture. Exact palette, typography, radii, shadow, iconography, imagery, and spacing values remain `TBD (Founder/Design Approval Required)`.

## Steel-Inspired Design Language

- **Structure:** modular, aligned, and load-bearing in appearance.
- **Surface:** restrained layers and edge definition rather than ornamental gloss.
- **Precision:** consistent dimensions, states, and alignment.
- **Reflection:** limited highlight or gradient treatment for emphasis, never persistent glare.
- **Strength:** clear hierarchy and durable components, not visual heaviness.
- **Craft:** carefully resolved Persian type, mixed-script details, and state behavior.

## Trust Language

Trust should come from visible process and verified information:

- Identify what is known, what is pending, and what requires inquiry.
- Use evidence-backed specifications and documents from their canonical sources.
- Make contact, support, privacy, and inquiry expectations clear.
- Do not use fabricated counters, customer logos, testimonials, certifications, availability, origin, guarantee, or project claims.
- Do not convert animation, badges, metallic effects, or iconography into implied evidence.

## Industrial Elegance

Industrial elegance is achieved through reduction and precision:

- One dominant hierarchy rather than multiple competing focal points.
- Controlled accents rather than full-surface effects.
- Clean technical tables/cards with meaningful grouping.
- Purposeful whitespace without reducing information density below B2B needs.
- Motion as feedback or orientation, with rare expressive moments.

## Persian UX Considerations

- Write primary UI labels in clear Persian; retain English internal keys only in administration or documented technical contexts.
- Preserve approved English grades, standards, dimensions, and brand terms where translation would reduce accuracy.
- Test Persian punctuation, half-spaces, numerals, units, line breaks, and mixed-direction strings.
- Avoid long all-caps Latin text and decorative Persian typography in controls or technical data.
- Use culturally neutral, professional imagery and language unless a specific approved audience requires otherwise.
- Design validation must include realistic long Persian labels and incomplete optional data.

## Brand Application Rules

- The brand layer never overrides content truth, accessibility, SEO semantics, or business rules.
- A component may use premium styling only after its purpose and state remain clear without decoration.
- Repeated patterns use the same terms and visual state logic across Blocksy and Elementor.
- Product-specific finish or color language comes from Product Data, not from brand styling.
- Founder approval is required before final tokens, logo rules, imagery, claims, or public copy are adopted.
